What Are These Tests?

IELTS

The IELTS (International English Language Testing System) is a globally recognized English proficiency test required for admission, work, or migration to countries like the UK, Canada, and Australia. It assesses listening, reading, writing, and speaking skills. Students applying to programs such as Nursing, Public Health, or Business are often required to submit IELTS scores. Anyone aged 16 or older can register. Both Academic and General Training formats depends on your purpose.

Visit Official IELTS Website

SAT

The SAT is a standardized test primarily used for undergraduate admissions in the United States. It measures knowledge in reading, writing, and math, and is typically taken by high school juniors and seniors. Many U.S. universities consider it alongside GPA and extracurriculars. It‚Äôs commonly required for degrees such as Computer Science, Engineering, or Business Administration. While some schools are test-optional, SAT scores can strengthen applications, especially for scholarships.

Visit Official SAT Website

GRE

The GRE (Graduate Record Examination) is required by many graduate and business schools globally, especially in the U.S. and Canada. It evaluates verbal reasoning, quantitative reasoning, and analytical writing. Applicants to programs like Data Science, MBA, Economics, or Engineering often submit GRE scores. There are no strict eligibility criteria it‚Äôs open to anyone planning postgraduate study. Many top universities use GRE scores to assess academic scholarship potential.
