Rubrics

Your SAT essay will be evaluated based on five essential criteria. These are designed to reflect the standards of academic writing expected in college-level tests and practice exams. Each essay receives a single writing score between 1 and 8 based on the following elements:

  • Command of Evidence: The relevance and strength of the examples and references used to support your analysis.
  • Focus and Coherence: The clarity of your central idea and the logical flow and organization of your essay.
  • Style and Formal Tone: Use of academic language, formal tone, and appropriate word choices without slang.
  • Grammar and Usage: Accuracy in sentence structure, punctuation, and grammar throughout the response.
  • Vocabulary & Sentence Variety: Use of varied sentence structures and vocabulary that enhances clarity and engagement.
